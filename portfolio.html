Portfolio

The projects listed here represent only a fraction of my overall experience as I am not able to share many of my most interesting and challenging projects due to privacy agreements, works in progress, or public availability. While many of these sites were developed in a team environment, I have attributed each project to the company that developed the site and have described my contributions. Feel free to contact me regarding my role in each project, or any questions you may have.

FaithVillage.com

Developed By: LiveOak360.com

FaithVillage is a social network for faith experiences that uniquely puts community, content and commerce in one convenient place. An animated visual interface enables users to experience a virtual village of content located in buildings representing sections of the site. The site launched as a resource blog in early 2011 and again in late 2012 with a rich array of resources and social media capabilities for churches, publishers, non-profits and general users.

My contributions included development on on all aspects on the codebase which included video/audio ingest, search engine, social network aspects (sharing, liking, commenting, walls, newsfeeds, etc). Stack included Zend Framework, Amazon AWS/S3, Doctrine 2 ORM, Sphinx search server, MooTools, MySQL, LessCSS, OpenX, and more.

Site Features
  • Full social network stack - sharing, commenting, liking, messaging, etc.
  • Modeling of large amounts of interactions for friendships, walls, feeds, etc.
  • Integration with many APIs and other platforms
  • User generated content including ingesting video, audio, and blogs
  • Complete admin, blogging, and content management systems

Communify (formerly BearHug)

Developed By: LiveOak360.com

MyCommunify is a support app on steroids. It combines status updates (Pulse), an interactive question app (Answers), a twitter based app for your company (OrgTweet), a rating and review module (FirstRate), SMS messaging, email notifications, branded urls and custom subdomains, beta features too early to disclose, and maybe an alien death ray. This app truly embraces the customer, and has been as fun to build as it has to use.

My contributions included development on the Pulse module, FirstRate module, SMS integration, email notifications, admin sections, public users, db fun, twitter oAuth, bug fixes, and various unreleased features.

Site Features
  • SMS/Email/twitter notifications
  • Custom subdomain and branded urls
  • Multiple subscription levels
  • User generated content and feedback
  • Module and user management

BigAssFans.com

Developed By: creativelink

Big Ass Fan Co. is the preeminent designer and manufacturer of high volume/low speed (HVLS) ceiling and vertical fans designed to improve facility and worker comfort in large commercial, industrial, agricultural and institutional buildings worldwide.

I assumed the role of lead developer for this project, using large portions of their previous CMS as a base, and converted the client's design into the site you see. This site was written using the CodeIgniter MVC framework and contains plenty of jQuery flavored javascript.

Site Features
  • CodeIgniter PHP Framework
  • jQuery javascript library
  • Page specific Video/Photo galleries
  • User uploaded multimedia content
  • Social media aspects
  • Database driven front-end
  • Rich UI/UX
  • Custom CMS

ArmyBaylor.com

Developed By: JDStraughan

Since the Army-Baylor Alumni Association's inception in 1947 they have contributed a diverse group of professionals skilled in the delivery of health care to the field of medicine. Graduates of the program come from all around the world and various services: Army, Navy, Air Force, Coast Guard and Veterans Affairs. Graduates leave the Fort Sam Houston, Texas campus and go off to serve our Nation. The new site highlights the successes of the past and propels the association into 2010.

I was honored to be a part of this project. God Bless our Troops

Site Features
  • Javascript Photo Gallery / Slideshow
  • Social media links and valid markup
  • Database driven front-end
  • Managed PayPal links for events / dues
  • Custom CMS with multiple user levels
  • Drag and drop ordering in CMS
